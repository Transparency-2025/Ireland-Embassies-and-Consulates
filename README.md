# Definitions and Lists of Diplomatic Missions in Ireland

A comprehensive list of diplomatic missions, such as Embassies and Consulates etc, in Ireland

### **Definitions of Diplomatic Missions**

Before viewing the contact list, it is helpful to understand the differences between the types of missions, as this affects the services they can provide.

* **Embassy:** The primary diplomatic representation of one country in another, located in the capital city (Dublin). It is headed by an **Ambassador**.
    * *Services:* Handles major political relations, trade, full visa processing, and serious emergency assistance for its citizens.
* **Consulate / Consulate General:** A satellite office of the Embassy, often located in major cities outside the capital (though sometimes in the capital if no full Embassy exists). It is headed by a **Consul** or **Consul General**.
    * *Services:* Focuses on citizen services (passports, documents) and trade. It typically has fewer diplomatic powers than an Embassy but full bureaucratic capability.
* **Honorary Consulate:** A less formal office headed by an **Honorary Consul**, often a private citizen (Irish or foreign national) who performs consular duties part-time.
    * *Services:* **Limited.** They generally **cannot** issue visas or passports directly but can provide emergency forms, advice, and assistance to citizens in distress. They usually operate out of private business offices (e.g., law firms, shipping companies).
