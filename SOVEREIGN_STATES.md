This request requires compiling data from geopolitical, geographical, and business classification systems.

The list below includes **195 recognized sovereign states** (193 UN members plus the Holy See and Palestine) plus several **non-sovereign entities** (like principalities and territories), categorized by:
1.  **Continent** (Standard Geographical Model)
2.  **Region** (Standard Geographical/Political Model, e.g., UN or US State Department)
3.  **Business Region** (Common commercial classifications: **EMEA, NAM, LATAM, APAC**)

***

### **Key Regional Definitions**

| Code | Name | Includes | Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **EMEA** | **E**urope, **M**iddle **E**ast, & **A**frica | All of Europe, all of Africa, and Western/Central Asia. | The largest and most diverse business region. |
| **NAM** | **N**orth **A**merica | Primarily **United States** (US) and **Canada** (CA). | Also referred to as NA (North America). Often excludes Mexico. |
| **LATAM** | **L**atin **Am**erica | Mexico, Central America, the Caribbean, and all of South America. | Mexico is included here for business purposes, though geographically it is N. America. |
| **APAC** | **A**sia **P**acific | All of East Asia, Southeast Asia, South Asia, and Oceania/Australasia. | Very large, high-growth region. Sometimes split into APJ (Asia Pacific & Japan) or includes MEA (Middle East/Africa) for some companies. |
